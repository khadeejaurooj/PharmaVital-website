Project Title: Development of a Personalized Immunotherapy for Cancer

Scientist: Dr. Farah Khan, Research Scientist at PharmaVital
Project Goal: To develop a new approach to cancer treatment using immunotherapy, where a patient's own immune system is harnessed to fight their specific tumor.

Project Phases:
Phase 1: Biomarker Discovery and Patient Selection
* Dr. Khan would collaborate with immunologists and geneticists to identify unique genetic markers on a patient's tumor cells
* These markers would act as targets for the immune system to recognize and destroy the cancer cells.
* The project would involve developing a diagnostic test to identify patients whose tumors are suitable for this type of personalized therapy.

Phase 2: Engineering Immune Cells and Pre-clinical Testing
* Once tumor markers are identified, Dr. Khan would design a method to extract a patient's immune cells (T-cells) and engineer them to recognize and attack the specific markers on their cancer cells.
* This might involve gene editing techniques to enhance the T-cells' ability to target the tumor. * Pre-clinical studies would be conducted using cell cultures and animal models to assess the safety and efficacy of
the engineered T-cells.

Phase 3: Clinical Trials and Treatment Optimization
* If pre-clinical studies are successful, Dr. Chen would work with clinical trial specialists to design protocols for testing the personalized immunotherapy in a small group of patients.
* The project would involve monitoring patients' responses and refining the T-cell engineering process for better treatment outcomes.

Project Timeline: Similar to the Alzheimer's project, this could be a multi-year endeavor with uncertain success at each stage. Personalized medicine is a growing field with the potential to revolutionize cancer treatment.
Dr. Khan's Role: Dr. Khan would leverage her expertise in immunology, cell biology, and potentially gene editing techniques. She would be responsible for designing experiments, analyzing patient data, collaborating with a cross-functional team, and
ensuring the project adheres to ethical and regulatory guidelines.
