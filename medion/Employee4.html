Project Title: Implementing a New Dissolution Testing Method for a Sustained-Release Painkiller Tablet

Scientist: Dr. Sarah Gill, Quality Control Analyst: at PharmaVital
Project Goal: To validate and implement a new dissolution testing method for a sustained-release painkiller tablet to ensure consistent drug release over time.

Project Phases:
Phase 1: Method Development and Validation
* Ms. Sarah Gill would research and review current dissolution testing methods for sustained-release tablets.
* She would collaborate with analytical chemists to develop a new method that accurately measures the drug's release profile at specific time intervals, mimicking its behavior in the body.
* Validation experiments would be conducted to demonstrate the new method's accuracy, precision, and robustness (reproducibility under slight variations).

Phase 2: Implementation and Ongoing Monitoring * Once validated, Ms. Sarah Gill would create detailed standard operating procedures (SOPs) for the new dissolution testing method ensuring its consistent application by all analysts.
* She would train other QC personnel on the new method and ensure all equipment is properly calibrated and functioning correctly.
* Ms. Gill would then integrate the new method into the routine testing process for every batch of the painkiller tablets produced.
* She would analyze the dissolution data and flag any batches that fall outside the established parameters, potentially triggering investigations or product rejection.

Project Timeline:
This project would likely take several months to complete. While seemingly smaller in scale than research projects, it's crucial for ensuring patient safety and product efficacy.
Ms. Gill's Role:Ms. Gill would utilize her knowledge of pharmaceutical analysis, quality control procedures, and regulatory requirements. Her key responsibilities would be:
* Researching and developing the method
* Performing validation experiments
* Creating SOPs and training personnel
* Implementing the method in routine testing
* Analyzing data and reporting any deviations
